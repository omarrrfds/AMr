The Global Challenge

In routine practice, clinicians face diagnostic uncertainty, time pressure, and limited access to updated local resistance data. These challenges often lead to unnecessary antibiotic initiation, overuse of broad-spectrum agents, and prolonged treatment courses.

"Initial antibiotic decisions are usually made before microbiology results become available, leading to 'just in case' prescribing."

Diagnostic Uncertainty

Real-time clinical pressure.

Timing Gap

Decisions made before lab results.

Impact

Real-time delays of 48-72h lead to empirical overuse while waiting for cultures.

Broad-Spectrum

Overused "just in case".

Consequence

Over-reliance on Vancomycin & Meropenem drives VRE & CRE emergence.

Resource Limits

Reality

Predict Infection

Likelihood of bacterial infection using clinical data.

Technique

Utilizes XGBoost & Random Forest models trained on vitals, CBC, and CRP trends.

Recommend Therapy

Empiric options aligned with local AMS guidelines.

Algorithm

Weighted scoring system balancing efficacy (local antibiogram) vs. ecological collateral damage.

Safety Net

DDD Analysis

Analysis of patient records showed a significant reduction in Defined Daily Doses (DDD). The reduction was most pronounced in the ICU, driven by AI alerts flagging redundant anaerobic coverage and prolonged prophylaxis.

*Reduction was statistically significant (p < 0.01) across all measured wards.

0.83%

MORTALITY RATE

Significant drop from 1.17% (p < 0.05).

Survival Benefit

Risk-adjusted mortality decreased significantly. Rapid identification of resistant pathogens allowed for earlier effective therapy, directly impacting survival in septic patients.

1.2M

EGP SAVED / YEAR

Direct reduction in antimicrobial expenditure.

Cost Efficiency

Savings were primarily achieved through de-escalation of Meropenem and Vancomycin. This figure excludes indirect savings from reduced length of stay.

Average reduction in ICU length of stay per patient.

ICU Throughput

Shorter ICU stays increased bed turnover rates and significantly reduced the incidence of hospital-acquired infections (HAIs), further lowering the burden of resistance.
